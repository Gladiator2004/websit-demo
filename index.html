About deMITasse Energies

Pioneering the Future of Sustainable Energy Storage

Our Mission & Story

deMITasse Energies is dedicated to solving humanity's greatest challenge – climate change. Founded by three passionate engineers, we have evolved from a spare bedroom startup into a dynamic team of sustainability-focused professionals based in New Delhi and New Jersey. We develop innovative long-duration energy storage systems that enable true renewable energy adoption, making the critical transition from fossil fuels both achievable and economically viable.

Our Technology

We are developing and commercializing our cutting-edge thermo-chemical technology that enables our plants to efficiently capture and store energy, up to grid scales for several months with zero energy dissipation, at a price point that is 4-8 times cheaper than other alternatives including lithium ion batteries. Thermo-chemical energy storage involves chemical reactions to store and release thermal energy – after nuclear, it's the most effective and energy-dense way to store energy.

Proprietary Innovation & Commercial Deployment

At the heart of a deMITasse plant is our thermo-chemical cycle, powered by an intricate interplay of our proprietary chemicals and catalysts. After years of intense research and testing, we have developed the first deployable version of our technology. We are now implementing learnings from successful paid pilots across industries to begin commercial deployment in the US and Indian markets by mid-2024, with limited pre-orders available for TRL-6/7 pilot plants.

Our Offering & Industry Impact

Since 2018, we've collaborated with industry leaders across power, chemical, textile, cement, steel, and shipbuilding sectors. Our primary offering is MW-scale grid-interacting energy storage plants that time-shift off-peak electricity and provide mechanisms for "just transition". These plants enable power grids to operate with extreme reliability in volatile environments, ultimately enabling replacement of all fossil-fueled power plants with zero emission alternatives.
